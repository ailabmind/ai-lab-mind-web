Excuses we tell ourselves not to use AI

Reading time: 4 min
Category: Common Errors
Publication date: 2024-05-18
Author: Lilly Valiente
Passionate about using AI to free up time, not to complicate your life.

It's not that AI doesn't work. It's that sometimes it's applied incorrectly.

When computers first appeared, many thought they would replace jobs. And they weren't wrong. What they did misinterpret was who would lose those jobs: it wasn't the machine itself that replaced people... it was another person who did learn to use it.

Today, the computer is an essential part of our daily lives. Can you imagine writing a report by hand? Or sending a letter by physical mail for an urgent meeting? We adapted because there was no alternative.

The same thing is happening with Artificial Intelligence. Fear returns, excuses return:

  • "That's not for me."
  • "I'm more of a human person."
  • "I don't want to be replaced."

And without realizing it, we become the kind of person who got left behind... by clinging to what's known.

It's not too late, but it's not too early either

If you're reading this, you still have time. But not for long. Every day that passes, there's someone in your industry who is already using AI to save time, reduce errors, increase their income... or simply to sleep better.

It's not about becoming a software engineer. It's about learning to think with AI. Knowing what to ask it. Understanding what it's for and what it's not. And above all, stopping making excuses.

“Because AI won't replace you. But someone who knows how to use it probably will.”

You are not here to be employed by your business.
You are here to lead it.

Automating isn't about becoming technical. It's about becoming free. Start with one thing. AI will do the rest.
